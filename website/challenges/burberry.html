Burberry brand challenge

Burberry's board has earmarked £100m for one strategic growth investment over the next 18 months. You're the new Chief Strategy Officer. Recommend the bet. 1,000 words.

The question

Burberry stabilised in 2026 after the worst trading year in a decade. Adjusted operating profit recovered from £26m to £160m. The board has signed off £100m of growth capital for the next 18 months. They have not yet decided where it goes.

You're the new Chief Strategy Officer. The CEO wants your recommendation, in 1,000 words. Where does the £100m go, and why this bet over the alternatives?

The shortlist the board has discussed

You don't have to pick from this list, but the board has explicitly debated each. If you pick one of these, justify why over the others. If you pick something else, justify why over all of them.

  • A. Build leather goods at the €1,200-1,800 price band — the segment Burberry vacated.
  • B. Reset US wholesale + open one US flagship.
  • C. Owned-store expansion in tier-2 Mainland Chinese cities.
  • D. Acquire or build a digital-first sister brand for under-30 customers.
  • E. Hire a marquee creative talent + a relaunch collection.
  • F. Return capital via buyback to support the share price during the recovery.

How it's judged

Every submission is scored on a public rubric. Weights sum to 100%.

  • Diagnosis (25%). Have you identified the right problem this bet solves?
  • Decisiveness (30%). Have you committed to one bet and explicitly rejected the alternatives?
  • Rigour (25%). Do the numbers stack up? Have you used the Brief Pack data?
  • Craft (20%). Is it tightly argued and well-written within 1,000 words?

Scoring runs in two stages. A language model does a first pass against the rubric. The top 20 go to a live defence with three retired UK luxury and retail leaders, each candidate presenting for 30 minutes.

You can use AI. We expect you to. Use any tools. This isn't a test of whether you can write a memo without ChatGPT. It's a test of whether you can use AI well and add judgement on top.

The prize

Top performers are fast-tracked to interviews with launch partner employers across UK luxury retail, strategy consulting, and consumer-sector finance. Your published Writeup, with the panel's written verdict attached, sits permanently on your Mantle profile — a CV line you can link from LinkedIn or take into any interview.

The brief

You are the new Chief Strategy Officer of Burberry Group plc. The CEO has asked you a single question:

"The board has signed off £100m of growth capital over the next 18 months. We've debated where it goes for two months and we still don't agree. I need your recommendation. One bet. Not a portfolio. Tell me where the £100m goes, why this and not the alternatives, and how it pays back. Be specific. 1,000 words on my desk by Monday."

You inherit a stable business: revenue around £2.4bn, operating margin recovering to ~7%, market cap of about £4.5bn, Daniel Lee still in the creative director seat. The Burberry Forward strategy is in the market and analyst-credible. This £100m is the first real bet of the post-Schulman era.

The six options the board has discussed

None of these is "right". Each has live arguments for and against. Pick one — or propose something else — and defend it.

  • A. Leather goods at the €1,200-1,800 sweet spot. Schulman publicly said pricing went "too high." Burberry vacated the €1,200-1,800 handbag band — where Coach, Polène, Mansur Gavriel, and the entry-luxury LVMH bags sit. Reclaim it. Capex on product development + targeted store merchandising + accessory-led marketing.
  • B. US wholesale reset + one US flagship. Upgraded shop-in-shops at Saks, Bloomingdale's, Nordstrom, Galeries Lafayette have outperformed some Burberry-owned US doors. Double down: drive in-store environments at the top 8 wholesale partners and open a single statement US flagship (NYC SoHo or Beverly Hills).
  • C. Tier-2 Mainland China owned-store expansion. Greater China is back to +4% growth. The Chinese luxury consumer is increasingly buying domestically (not just on travel). Build 12-15 new owned stores in Chengdu, Hangzhou, Xi'an, Nanjing, Suzhou. Capex-heavy but sticky local revenue.
  • D. Acquire or build a digital-first sister brand. Burberry's customer base is older and getting older. £100m buys a small, hot DTC brand for under-30s (Reformation-scale) or builds one from scratch. Manage as a separate house, share supply chain.
  • E. Marquee creative hire + relaunch collection. Lee is still creative director but persistent rumours link him to Jil Sander. £100m funds a Phoebe Philo, Daniel Roseberry, or similar — plus a fully-funded relaunch collection, runway show, marketing campaign. Reset the brand narrative.
  • F. £100m buyback. Shares trade at ~10x forward earnings, well below LVMH's 22x. A buyback supports the price during the fragile recovery, signals confidence to the market, and protects the share count against the persistent Moncler/LVMH bid speculation. Boring but defensible.

Suggested structure (1,000 words)

You aren't required to follow this, but submissions that hit each section tend to score higher. Word counts are guidance, not caps.

  • The bet (1 sentence, ~30 words). Your one-line recommendation.
  • Why this, not the others (~300 words). Specifically why your pick beats two or three of the alternatives. Don't just argue for your bet — argue against the obvious counterproposal.
  • How it pays back (~250 words). Where Burberry is in 36 months if you're right. Revenue lift, margin impact, market-cap re-rate. Numbers, not adjectives.
  • What you'd stop doing to fund it (~200 words). Where does the £100m come from inside Burberry, and what gets cut, paused, or deprioritised.
  • First 90 days (~200 words). The three or four specific moves you make in the first quarter to put the bet in motion.

What the panel will push you on

The top 20 face a live defence. Expect questions like:

  • "If your bet is right, why hasn't LVMH or Kering done the same with their adjacent brands?"
  • "What's the single number that would prove you wrong in 18 months?"
  • "Convince me why option [X] isn't actually the right answer."

You don't have to read every source — but a serious submission visibly uses the data and the panel will check.

Strategic facts you can build on

  • Market cap recovery. Trough ~£2.2bn (Sep 2024) at 575p. Recovery ~£4–5bn by mid-2026 after the +86% twelve-month run. Peak (post-COVID 2021) was above £10bn.
  • Cost programme. £100m annualised cost savings targeted by 2027. £80m delivered in FY26. 1,700 role reductions announced FY25.
  • Store estate. ~410 directly operated stores at end FY26. 21 closed, 9 opened in FY26.
  • Pricing. Schulman publicly: pricing went "too high across the board"; €1,600 handbag sweet spot.
  • Wholesale. Upgraded in-store environments at Saks, Bloomingdale's, Nordstrom, Galeries Lafayette outperforming some owned doors.
  • FTSE. Demoted from FTSE 100 September 2024. Re-promoted September 2025.
  • Chair succession. Gerry Murphy to be succeeded by William Jackson (Bridgepoint founder), effective November 2026.
  • Acquisition speculation. Moncler-Burberry tie-up was floated by media late 2024. Moncler publicly denied. LVMH owns 15.8% of Moncler's controlling shareholder.

Industry context (Bain–Altagamma, 2025)

  • Global personal luxury market: ~$363bn in 2025, down on prior year.
  • Luxury consumer base: ~330m in 2025, down ~20m from prior year.
  • Volume growth across the category 2023–2025 was driven roughly 80% by price increases.
  • Hermès and LVMH widened the gap on the rest in the post-COVID cycle. Kering (Gucci) is the cleanest negative parallel to Burberry.

Source: Bain–Altagamma Luxury Goods Worldwide Market Study 2025

Opt-out

You can ask for your submission to be unpublished or anonymised at any point, before or after results. The default is published; the opt-out is a checkbox at submission.

Eligibility

  • You must be 18 or over.
  • You must have the right to work in the UK (we cannot guarantee employer interviews if you do not).
  • You must be currently looking for graduate-level employment. Final-year students and people in their first three years of work both qualify.
  • No prior fashion or luxury experience required. Mantle is about ability, not background.

Use of AI

You can and should use AI tools. ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, any of them. AI is part of how modern analytical work gets done and the test is whether you can use it well.

You will be asked, at the live defence stage, to talk through your process. If you cannot explain or defend your own submission, you will not advance. That is the only AI-use rule that actually matters.

Submission format

  • 1,000 words maximum. Submissions over the limit are penalised. The word count includes everything in the body — exclude footnotes, source citations, and chart captions.
  • One Writeup, single document. PDF or Markdown, no larger than 10 MB.
  • Up to 4 charts or tables. Embedded inline. Cover image required at the top of the Writeup.
  • No appendix. 1,000 words is the test. If it doesn't fit, cut.
  • Deadline. 23:59 GMT on 30 Jul 2026.
  • One submission per person. You can save versions until the deadline. Only the most recent submitted version counts.

Originality and authorship

The Writeup must be your own work. You can use AI freely as a tool. You cannot pay or ask another human to write it for you. If you cannot defend the contents at the live panel, you fail out regardless of leaderboard position.

Public submissions

The top 20 submissions are published publicly with the author's name and the panel's written verdict, unless the author opts out. This is the credential. The judges' verdict on your work is what you carry into the rest of your career. See the Submissions tab for full detail on what gets published.
