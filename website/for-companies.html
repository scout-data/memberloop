Problem

Your funnel selects against
the people you actually want.

140 graduates apply for every junior role. You can't read 140 applications. So the pile gets cut by signals that have nothing to do with the job: which university, which internship, which name on the CV. AI screeners have made it worse, not better. They strip out anyone who didn't write their CV with the right keywords.

The graduates who'd transform a small team are not the ones with the polished application. They're the ones who'd absorb a real brief in a weekend, push back on the question, and ship something better than you asked for. That doesn't show up on a CV. It can't.

Meanwhile entry-level hiring in law, consulting, finance, marketing, and journalism is down up to 35% in 18 months. The roles that remain are more important than ever. Every hire has to count. And the funnel you've inherited is optimised for the opposite, throughput, not signal.

You need to see the work before you see the CV.

Where it fits

Plugs into the hiring you
already run.

Mantle doesn't replace your ATS or your interview process. It replaces the top of your funnel, the bit you currently outsource to a CV pile.

  1. 1 140 applicants drop CVs into a portal
